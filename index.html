On AI in marketing

Accelerate the strategy.
Never replace it.

For early-stage teams with tight budgets and small crews, the right AI approach isn't a nice-to-have — it's a real competitive lever. The founders who figure out how to use it well move faster, stretch further, and punch above their weight. But only if the strategy underneath it is sound.

  • AI propagates great messaging — it doesn't generate it
  • Efficiency only compounds when the foundation is right
  • The best AI-enabled marketing still sounds like a human with a point of view
About

The person
behind the work.

I've spent my career as a marketing executive at early-stage B2B SaaS companies — holding CMO and VP-level roles full-time, and more recently as a fractional leader across several engagements.

The work I find most meaningful is the strategic side — helping founders get clear on who they're really selling to, why buyers choose them over the alternative, and how to build a marketing motion that actually feeds the pipeline. That clarity tends to unlock everything else.

I chose the fractional model deliberately. After years as a full-time marketing leader at companies like yours, I kept seeing the same pattern: founders wanted — and needed — senior marketing experience, but committing to a full-time executive salary at the early stage is often the wrong investment. Fractional gives you the experience without the overhead. And if I'm honest, it also changes the dynamic. When I'm a partner rather than an employee, founders tend to engage more openly and collaboratively — and that usually leads to better outcomes.

SymTrain

From "here's what we do" to "here's what you get" — and a pipeline to show for it.

The situation

SymTrain had a strong product but their positioning led with features and functionality — explaining how the platform works rather than the value it delivers. It was a story built for internal clarity, not buyer conviction.

What we did

We rebuilt their messaging and positioning from the ground up with a focus on customer outcomes and ROI — brought to life across a new website, sales enablement materials, and an ROI calculator designed to move deals forward.

The outcome

Inbound lead flow increased, and the new materials are now being used to drive expansion within existing accounts — turning a messaging overhaul into a tool for both new business and revenue growth.

Pulsate & CometChat

Marketing finally had a number to own — and sales finally had leads worth calling.

The situation

It's a pattern I've seen more than once. Marketing generating activity with no accountability to revenue, no visibility into lead sources, and good leads slipping through the cracks unworked while sales wasted time on the wrong ones.

What we did

We rebuilt their HubSpot infrastructure from the ground up — full visibility across every lead source, fixed handoff flows, and a lead scoring model that helped sales prioritize the right conversations at the right time.

The outcome

Qualified leads stopped falling through the cracks. Fewer deals stalled at the starting line. The marketing-to-sales handoff shortened. And overall product demos increased — a direct reflection of a pipeline that was better qualified and better managed.
